Benefits of recycling

Here are the facts:

Conserves natural resources

Fossil fuel consumption has rapidly increased in the last 200 years, leaving reserves depleted.

Recycling plastic and other materials are beneficial because they save trees, fossil fuels, and raw materials.

Cuts climate-changing carbon emissions

For every ton of plastic that is produced, three tons of CO2 emissions are created.

Recyling means using less energy and less sourcing for new materials. This lowers carbon emissions and keeps any methane-releasing waste out of landfill sites.

Saves energy

A one-liter plastic bottle uses about two liters of water during this process.

Making products from recycled materials require a lot less energy than creating them from new raw materials.

Protects ecosystems and wildlife

7 billion trees are cut down each year.

Reduces the number of harmful disruptions and damages being done to the natural world: less deforestation, less animals harmed or displaced, and less pollution of water, air and soil.
